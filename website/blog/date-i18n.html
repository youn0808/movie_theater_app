Retrospective: The Do's and Don'ts of Developing

Retrospective: The Do's and Don'ts of Developing

  • March 31, 2021
  • Posted by John Doe
  • Development

We learned a lot through the development process of our app, taking away both positive and critical lessons over the course of the semester. Here is a list of Do’s and Don’ts we discovered through the process:

Do Not: Leave merging major features to the last couple days.

Our group fell victim to this issue in Iteration 1, meaning our last day was stressful just to get everything online in time. This issue was a learning process for many of us who had not used Git previously or worked in such a cooperative manner before - although a welcomed lesson.

Do: Merge early and merge often

Aim to leave yourself a good couple of days for only merging/fixing “come-up” events. For iteration 2, we really improved this aspect. We aimed to have major features relatively complete early within the week the iteration was due. This allowed us the time to comb over our product for weird bugs and UI issues that we may not have been able to catch had we not been finished major functions earlier in the week.

Do Not: Expect your time estimates to be that close to the actual time

As with many other groups, our time estimates for dev tasks and features were off by quite a bit. For iteration 1, part of the reason we were rushed can be partially attributed to the sheer amount of work we billeted for ourselves in the work period. Going off our time assumptions it should have been easy to finish everything in timely fashion - although our estimates were off in the first place, causing us to bite off a bit more than we maybe should have.

Do: Review your time estimates from previous iterations - and use this retrospective

Through retrospective we can formalize how we are doing well or falling short in development. Through retrospective we were able to better estimate workloads and distribute accordingly.
